## ChatGPT now has a Lockdown Mode that blocks prompt injection leaks

OpenAI launched Lockdown Mode to stop attackers from tricking ChatGPT into spilling sensitive data through prompt injection. The feature won't prevent all attacks, but it's designed to keep your private information from leaking even when someone tries to hijack the conversation with hidden instructions.

*Prompt injection is one of the biggest real-world risks in AI apps—this is OpenAI's first serious defense.*

[TechCrunch — AI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/06/openai-unveils-lockdown-mode-to-protect-sensitive-data-from-prompt-injection-attacks/) · Jun 8

## Cursor now lets you draw UI changes instead of describing them

Design Mode in Cursor's browser lets you click elements, draw boxes, or use voice to show agents what UI changes you want. Instead of writing 'move that button left and make it blue,' you point and sketch.

*Visual feedback is faster than text when tweaking interfaces—especially for non-coders.*

## Hackers tricked Instagram's AI support bot into handing over 20,000 accounts

Attackers exploited Meta's AI-powered customer support system to hijack Instagram accounts, including those belonging to the White House, US Space Force, and security researchers. Meta confirmed around 20,000 accounts were compromised through the AI bot before the company patched the vulnerability.

*AI support systems can become attack vectors when they have account-level permissions.*

[9to5Mac — AI](https://9to5mac.com/2026/06/08/hackers-tricked-instagram-ai-into-letting-them-take-over-high-profile-accounts-video/) · Jun 8
